## Preliminary data and background
Genetic variation is not spread uniformly across the genome. Some regions have elevated rates of variation.

In regions with higher rates of variation you are more likely to have reference induced distortions.

By identify where use of the human genome reference fails to represent the donor haplotype, we can identify systemic gaps in our methods and by exploring known functions of such regions, we can get a sense of what sort of biology is implicated.

These previously obscured pockets of the genome, contain within them genes enriched for cellular signalling, cell surface, cellular adhesion. Processes that deal with how cells interact with their environment.

The are enriched for expression by Neurological, immunological, and progenetitor cells.

A very preliminary analysis of these regions suggests that genes associated with sensory cilia are significantly enriched for these regions of elevated variation.

The 1000G project also found that some pathways have a higher non-synonymous mutational burden (coding variants). Namely ECM-receptor interaction, DNA replication, and pentose phosphate pathway.

My analysis is not constrained to or focused on coding variation.

My interest lies in variation in the regulatory landscape in humans.

How does the elevated natural variation in regions encompassing cellular interaction mechanisms get tolerated? How does such variation impact cellular cross talk?

Capturing genomic function and sequence in these spaces has historically been undermined by the limitations of the human genome reference.

Natural processes underlying neurodegeneration, immune function, and tissue regeneration all have strong associations with cellular cross talk

They may also be one of the places where we see substantial epistasis (as in mouse study). This is very much in line with how I think about epistasis.

So the disease/translational implications could be great. The advent and growing availibility of iPSC lines from diverse genetic ancestries combined with advancements in genomics analysis to be able to handle genetic diversity makes exploration of these questions feasible at an unheard of scale and resolution.
